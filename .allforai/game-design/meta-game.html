01 Island Map — Visual State System

The island map is the meta-game's primary surface. Six restoration areas are laid out in a loose chain from the harbor up to the cliff and lighthouse. The lighthouse is always visible on the horizon regardless of chapter — it is the game's north star, never locked from view, never reachable until Chapter 6.

Map schematic — Ch1 & Ch2 shown as restored (stirring/blooming indicators active). Ch3–5 shown locked. Lighthouse always on horizon. Tap any area for restoration progress.

02 Chapter Visual States — Per-Area Detail

Each area transitions through four distinct visual states as the player progresses through restoration stages. Each radiant state features the ONE defining visual moment that marks the chapter's completion.

CHAPTER 01 漁港 Harbor 浮木 Driftwood
Desolate

Splintered docks, half-submerged boats. Tangled nets. Broken lantern poles. Grey-white fog at water's edge. Fish-market awning torn.

Stirring

One lantern re-hung, flickering. One boat upright and cleaned. Driftwood cleared from dock boards. Muted sea-blue returns.

Blooming

Three boats moored neatly. Fish-market stalls open, dusty-red awnings. Two fishermen NPCs move the dock. Seagulls overhead. Teal water.

Radiant

All lanterns alight at dusk. Amber light in dancing water columns. Market serves grilled fish. Fisherman NPC watches light on the water.

Radiant Moment 夕暮れ時、漁港の灯籠がひとつひとつ灯る。黄金色の光が水面に揺れる。 Fishing lanterns ignite one by one at dusk. Warm amber reflects in long rippling columns across the water.
CHAPTER 02 陶芸工房 Pottery Studio 陶土 Clay
Desolate

Cold, cracked kiln. Pottery shards on floor. Dusty half-thrown forms on shelves. Wheel dried hard. Door on one hinge.

Stirring

Floor swept. Wheel turning slowly, cleaned. Fresh clay mound beside the wheel. Kiln exterior patched. Earthy tones return.

Blooming

Three shelves of glazed pottery (soft blue + cream). Potter works at wheel, focused. Second NPC stacks pieces. Display table outside. Leaf-green palette.

Radiant

Kiln fires up. Door ajar, deep orange light pulses out. Heat shimmer from chimney. Potter pauses, bows slightly toward kiln. Private moment.

Radiant Moment 窯に火が入る。扉から深い橙色の光が溢れ出す。熱気が立ち上り、島に陶芸の香りが戻ってくる。 The kiln fires. Deep orange light floods the courtyard. For the first time in years, the island smells of warm clay and wood-smoke.
CHAPTER 03 海辺の森 Coastal Forest 苔と種 Moss & Seeds
Desolate

Path invisible under choked undergrowth. Rusted signs. Fallen branches. Dark blue-green shadow. Light barely penetrates canopy.

Stirring

Narrow path cleared (single-file width). Bright moss at path edges. One seedling in cleared patch reaching for filtered light.

Blooming

Wide defined path. Young ferns, placed stones at edges. Two NPC children running through — deliberate echo of 渉's memory. Dappled gold light.

Radiant

Cherry blossoms in full bloom create a canopy arch. Petals drift continuously. Soft pink-white light. Cliff visible at path's end.

Radiant Moment 桜の花が一斉に開く。花びらがひなたの髪に舞い落ちる。彼女は気づかず、ただ光の中を見ている。 Cherry blossoms all open at once. A single petal lands on Hinata's hair. She doesn't notice — she's looking ahead at the light through the trees.
CHAPTER 04 石畳の小道 Stone Path 石畳石 Paving Stones
Desolate

Cracked, sunken paving stones. Toppled stone lanterns, cracked glass. Weeds in every joint. Path edges eroded. Cold grey-blue.

Stirring

First section levelled. One lantern re-erected, new glass (unlit but intact). Path edges trimmed. Hand-laid stonework beauty beginning to show.

Blooming

Most path restored and level. Lanterns upright with new glass at intervals. Elder NPC walks slowly, tapping cane. Deliberate moss in joints.

Radiant

All lanterns alight at twilight in sequence. Amber chain of light stretches full path length. Stones glow warm underfoot. Ancient + cared-for.

Radiant Moment 夕暮れに、石灯籠が一つずつ順番に灯る。その光の連なりが、森から崖まで続く。まるで星が地に落ちたような道。 Stone lanterns ignite in sequence at twilight — a chain of amber light stretching from forest to cliff. A landing strip for stars.
CHAPTER 05 崖の展望台 Cliff Lookout 崖の石英 Cliff Quartz
Desolate

Crumbling railing, missing sections. Rotted bench. Blank weathered plaques. Overcast sky perpetually — view present but grey and obscured.

Stirring

Dangerous railing blocked with rope. Bench cleared and sound. Quartz fragments at platform edge, catching light. Sky shows through cloud gaps.

Blooming

New stone railing with quartz inlays catching sunlight. Bench restored. NPC couple at the view. In the distance: the lighthouse on its rock.

Radiant

Night time-lapse. Quartz railing seems to glow from within. Stars emerge. Milky Way arcs overhead. Lighthouse beam sweeps past — first time.

Radiant Moment 星空のタイムラプス。石英の手すりが星の光を捕まえて静かに輝く。遠くから、灯台の光が初めて海を渡ってくる。 A star time-lapse plays. The quartz railing glows faintly with starlight. From the distance, the lighthouse beam sweeps across the sea for the first time — a promise of what is next.
CHAPTER 06 輝く灯台 Lighthouse 灯油 + 硝子
Desolate

Tower intact but dark. Cottage windows boarded. Path overgrown. Cold oil stains. Feels haunted — not merely abandoned. The loneliest structure.

Stirring

Path to door cleared. One cottage window unboarded — warm empty interior visible. Lens chamber accessed, cracked glass panels stacked. Small oil lanterns around base lit.

Blooming

Cottage inhabited — Hinata moves back in. Light in windows at night. New lens glass refracts daylight as prismatic colour. Beam not yet active.

Radiant

Beam activates. Warm gold sweeps across sea and island. Each prior chapter's signature glow caught for 1 second as the beam passes. The island is whole.

Radiant Moment — Game Climax 灯台の光が海に向かって放たれる。その光が漁港、工房、森、石畳、崖へと順番に触れていく。島全体が、ひとつの呼吸のように輝く。 The lighthouse beam sweeps across the sea. As it passes, each restored area — the harbor, the kiln, the blossoms, the stone path, the cliff quartz — glows for one second. The island breathes as one.

03 Chapter Unlock Ceremony — 6-Step Sequence

When a chapter's final level is completed, a six-step transition sequence plays. This ceremony is the emotional punctuation between chapters — it rewards completion while opening the next narrative window.

STEP 01 0 sec

Final Level Complete

Level complete screen fades. Star count is displayed briefly then fades to black. The screen holds black for 0.5s — a breath before the ceremony.

STEP 02 3–4 sec

Area Restoration Animation

Chapter N transitions to its radiant state with a full-area glow animation. The chapter's signature visual plays at full screen: harbor lanterns / kiln fire / cherry blossoms / stone lanterns / star time-lapse / lighthouse beam.

STEP 03 2 sec

Camera Pan to Adjacent Area

Camera pulls back to the island map, then pans to the adjacent locked area. Fog lifts in a soft 2-second dissolve to reveal the area silhouette. Colour is still muted — desolate state. Soft swell music: single koto note, rising.

STEP 04 5 sec

ひなた Hint Line

Hinata's portrait appears at lower third. She delivers a single Japanese line about what lies ahead — teasing the next chapter's theme without spoiling the story beat. Voiced with subtitle.

STEP 05 1 sec

Chapter Levels Unlock

New chapter area glows gold briefly on the map. Three level nodes appear with a soft unlocking chime — like a music-box note. The first 3 levels of the new chapter become playable immediately.

STEP 06 3 sec

Memory Fragment Unlock

A journal page floats up from the restored area with a paper-rustle sound effect. '渉の記憶 — 第N章' fades in briefly. The page folds into the Memory Room icon in the UI. Notification: 渉の記憶に新しい記録が加わりました。

ひなた's Chapter Transition Lines

One line per chapter transition. Each hints at the next area's emotional theme without spoiling the narrative. Poetic, personal, slightly hesitant.

Completionist Reward — Special Illustration

When all 6 fragments are collected, a full-page illustrated scene unlocks: 渉 and a young ひなた at the lighthouse, warm evening light. Drawn in the game's pastoral illustration style. The scene shows 渉 looking out at the sea with ひなた beside him, the letter already written but not yet sent.

Stage 1 Philosophy Affordable by level 5 of each chapter. Normal drop rate (2–5 units/level) accumulates ~15 materials in 4–5 plays. Instant gratification — the first visual transformation comes quickly.
Stage 2 Philosophy Requires ~half the chapter levels. Achievable after solid mid-chapter engagement. The second transformation rewards dedicated play without frustrating new players.
Stage 3 Philosophy Requires chapter completion + material surplus. The rare drop bonus (15% on 4+ chains) is what closes the gap. Players who play well are rewarded with early access to the radiant state.

07 Map Interaction Design

The island map is the player's home screen between sessions. It should feel warm, living, and inviting — not like a level-select menu. Every interaction is designed to reinforce the restoration narrative.

  • TAP RESTORED AREA Shows area name (JP/EN), current visual state, restoration percentage, stage completion indicators, and materials held vs. needed for next stage. A small animated vignette of the area plays at the centre of the panel.
  • TAP LOCKED AREA Foggy silhouette with area name visible (never invisible — the teaser is always present). A locked-chapter icon with chapter number. Tap again: "Complete [previous chapter name] to restore this area." Never hostile — curious and inviting.
  • TAP LIGHTHOUSE (Ch1–5) Lighthouse is visible on the map horizon but has no interactive tap target until Ch6. In Ch1–5, tapping near the lighthouse area triggers a soft visual pulse and ひなた's ambient voice line: 「いつかあそこまで、一緒に行けたらいいですね。」 (Someday, it would be nice to go there together.)
  • TAP LIGHTHOUSE (Ch6) Becomes a full interactive area with restoration panel. The lighthouse is treated like any other area — stages 1, 2, 3 — except that Stage 3 is the game's emotional climax and triggers the final animation.
  • FOG LIFT ANIMATION 2-second dissolve from fog to muted-colour silhouette. Fog dissolves from centre outward, like a held breath released. Soft swell music: a single sustained koto note rising. Plays during chapter unlock ceremony Step 3.
  • LONG-PRESS ANY AREA Opens a full-screen area view with ambient sound from that chapter. The area is rendered at full screen in its current visual state. Peaceful — like looking at a painting. Back-tap returns to map.
  • MEMORY ROOM ACCESS Top-right corner of island map. Icon is an aged journal. Shows sealed/unsealed envelope icons for each chapter's fragment. Tap unlocked fragment to view the journal page with 渉's handwriting and the animated page-turn.
  • RESTORATION PANEL Material display shows: material icon + quantity held + quantity needed for next stage. Visual progress bar per stage (empty → filled as materials accumulate). Spend button greys out when levels-required condition is not yet met — no frustrating "not enough materials" blocks, just a clear "Complete more levels first" state.

Lighthouse — The Horizon Rule

The lighthouse is visible in the game's horizon from the moment the player first sees the island map in Chapter 1. It is rendered at roughly 70% opacity, warm gold, on the right edge of the map. It does not respond to most taps. It does not have a chapter badge. It simply exists — a constant reminder of where the journey ends. This is deliberate. The player should always know where they are going, even if they cannot go there yet. The lighthouse is hope, visualised as an unreachable light on the horizon.

08 No Battle Pass — A Deliberate Design Decision

Glow Island has no Battle Pass.

No seasonal events. No expiring content. No daily login anxiety. No competitive leaderboards. No energy system. No timers. No PvP. These are not omissions — they are choices.

Battle Pass Seasonal Events Energy System Daily Login Bonus Expiring Content FOMO Mechanics Timers PvP Leaderboards Time-Gated Rewards

ERRC Framework — What We Eliminated, Reduced, Raised, Created

Eliminate
  • FOMO pressure from expiring content
  • Time-gated materials and rewards
  • Daily login anxiety and streaks
  • Competitive leaderboards and PvP stress
  • Energy systems that punish sustained play
  • Battle pass FOMO (fear of missing a tier)
  • Seasonal re-skinning that breaks the island's sense of a real place
Reduce
  • External motivation mechanisms (rewards for playing, not reasons to play)
  • Engagement as an obligation to the game
  • Monetisation friction in core restoration loop
  • Interruption of narrative flow by UI systems
Raise
  • Intrinsic motivation through narrative investment
  • Completionist satisfaction as primary engagement driver
  • Emotional weight of each restoration moment
  • Player trust — the game does not manipulate
  • The feeling that the island is a real place
Create
  • Finite narrative arc as the engagement spine
  • Memory fragment collection as the completionist hook
  • A game with a genuine ending — narrative closure
  • JOMO (Joy of Missing Out) — calm, unchased experience
  • No-pressure restoration loop that respects player time
Why Timeless > Seasonal
Seasonal content requires Kagayaki-jima to be periodically re-dressed for marketing events. In January it would wear new year's decorations. In October it would wear autumn leaves scheduled to expire on November 1st. This would destroy the most important thing about the island: the sense that it was always there, that it has real history, that the player's restoration work has permanent meaning. A battle pass would make 渉's story a backdrop for limited-time cosmetics. The lighthouse would become a banner ad for a season. We refuse this.
What Replaces FOMO: Completionist Satisfaction + Narrative Closure
FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) works by creating artificial scarcity. Limited-time content leverages anxiety as an engagement mechanism. Glow Island replaces this with a fundamentally different hook: the desire to know the end of the story. 渉's six journal entries are the most valuable content in the game, and they will never expire. The special illustration that unlocks when all six are collected will be available to every player, at every point in the future, who completes the restoration arc. This is not a soft replacement for FOMO — it is a direct rejection of it.
The Natural Finite Endpoint
The lighthouse beam activation is the game's intended ending. After this, the island is whole. The player has read 渉's final letter. They understand why he chose Ritsu. They have watched ひなた's face in the rotating light of the restored lamp room. The game does not extend past this with a post-game grind or new chapter unlock. There is no "Season 2." The island is complete. The player is free to revisit — to walk through every restored area, to re-read the memory fragments in order, to sit on the restored bench at the cliff lookout and watch the stars. But there is no pressure. The island will always be there. It will always be ready. Just as it waited for Ritsu, it will wait for you.
Long-Term Engagement from a Finite Experience
A game with a genuine ending creates something rare in mobile: recommendation value. Players who complete Glow Island will recommend it. They will say: "I finished it, it was beautiful, it has an ending, you should play it." This is a different engagement model from retention-at-all-costs. We are building a game that players finish with satisfaction rather than abandon from fatigue. The long-term engagement is not from the players who play indefinitely — it is from the players who finish, recommend, and return to replay from the beginning because they want to re-experience 渉's story knowing what they now know.
