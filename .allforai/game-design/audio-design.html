Glow Island · 輝島 · Audio Design Document

Audio Direction — 輝島 (Kagayaki-jima)

音楽と音響設計
A healing match-connect puzzle game set on a restored Japanese island.
Audio designed to hold silence as intentionally as it holds melody.
Section 01

Audio Identity Statement

The island does not demand your attention. It offers it, quietly. Every sound in 輝島 is chosen to deepen rest, reward presence, and make the player feel — not perform. Where pop puzzle games accelerate, we decelerate. Where others fill silence with urgency, we let silence breathe. The audio of Glow Island is the sound of a place slowly healing, and the player healing along with it.
Genre & Mood
Acoustic + Ambient + Light Electronic

Mood: Healing · Unhurried · Quietly Joyful

Aesthetic anchors: どうぶつの森 acoustic warmth · Joe Hisaishi space · Nujabes understated groove

Section 02

Adaptive Music System

Three independent audio layers stack in real-time to match player engagement without jarring transitions. The system always feels like the same piece of music — just more or less present.

Base
Layer
Always playing — environmental ambient sounds specific to each chapter area. Ocean waves, pottery wheel hum, forest wind, cliff wind, near-silence with room tone. This layer alone must feel appropriate and complete.
Volume: always on · Crossfade: 2s between chapters · Low-resource mode: only this layer plays
Melody
Layer
Fades in after 10s of active puzzle engagement — the primary musical theme for the chapter. Acoustic guitar in Ch1, Koto in Ch2, Piano in Ch3, Solo instrument in Ch4, ambient Koto in Ch5, progressive ensemble in Ch6.
Fade in: 3s · Fade out: 5s on pause or level exit · Trigger reset: each new level entry
Intensity
Layer
Activates on 3+ consecutive combo chains — adds subtle percussion or a secondary instrument. Should feel like the music notices the combo, not react frantically. Very subtle — never transforms the piece.
Fade in: 0.5s · Fade out: 3s after last combo · Volume: 60% of full · MUST feel like same piece

Transition Rules

Transition Rule Duration Exception
Chapter → Chapter Fade out current BGM, fade in new BGM 2s crossfade —
Menu → Chapter Menu BGM fades, chapter ambient starts 2s —
Level complete BGM ducks, level_complete sting plays, BGM resumes Auto via tween —
Lighthouse finale All BGM fades to silence, 2s pause, lighthouse_final plays 2s silence gap Never overlaps other BGM
Hard cuts NEVER. All transitions use tween-based fade. No hard audio cuts anywhere in the game.
Low Resource Mode: When player enables low-data mode, or device reports low battery, audio falls back to Base Layer only at lower bitrate. The base ambient layer is designed to feel complete even without melody — ocean waves and birdsong are music enough.

Section 04

Chapter BGM Design

Six chapter themes, each locked to a BGMKey in AudioConfig.ts. BPMs slow the emotional pace intentionally — this is not inattention, it is design.

Chapter 01 · Alpha
Harbor
漁港 (Ryokō) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_1
BPM 62
Key G major / E minor
Loop 3–4 min
Instruments: Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked) · Ocean wave field recording · Distant seagull · Wind chime accents · Sparse piano counter-melody
"Curious, slightly melancholic — arriving somewhere unfamiliar but quietly beautiful."
Morning light character. Music must have breathing gaps. Ocean waves audible in silences. Base layer = ocean. Melody layer fades in after 10s. Intensity = soft hand drum, barely there.
Chapter 02 · Alpha
Pottery Studio
陶芸工房 (Tōgei kōbō) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_2
BPM 72
Key D pentatonic
Loop 3–4 min
Instruments: Koto (primary, plucked patterns) · Soft hand percussion (clay-tapping 2-3 hit rhythm) · Pottery wheel hum (ambient) · Wooden block accent · Sparse guitar at phrase ends
"Focused, rhythmic — the meditative repetition of craft; hands working, mind quiet."
Rhythmic but never urgent. Hypnotic koto repetition. Clay-work rhythm is the heartbeat. Intensity layer makes hand percussion more prominent.
Chapter 03 · Alpha
Coastal Forest
海辺の森 (Umibe no mori) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_3
BPM 58
Key A major
Loop 4–5 min
Instruments: Soft piano (wide intervals, spacious) · Wind ambience (continuous) · Birdsong (multiple species) · Guitar harmonics (periodic) · Distant cicada · Heavy reverb on all melodic elements
"Wonder, expansive — stepping into dappled light; the forest is ancient and welcoming."
Most spacious chapter. Piano notes must fully decay before next note. Heavy reverb suggests cathedral forest canopy. Long 4-5 min loop to avoid repetition. Birdsong variation ensures freshness.
Chapter 04 · Alpha
Stone Path
石畳の小道 (Ishidatami no komichi) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_4
BPM 52
Key C major / A minor
Loop 5–6 min
Instruments: Solo piano (sparse, widely-spaced chords) OR Solo acoustic guitar — choose one · Distant wind (very low mix) · Occasional geta-on-stone steps · Room tone (silence filler)
"Introspective, quiet — walking alone with thoughts; finding peace in solitude."
MOST MINIMAL. Long intentional silences. Underlying room tone prevents audio dropout feeling. Do NOT fill the space. Intensity layer = adds second instrument very softly.
Chapter 05 · Beta
Cliff Lookout
崖の展望台 (Gake no tenbōdai) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_5
BPM 68
Key F# minor
Loop 5–6 min
Instruments: Layered ambient pads (slowly evolving) · Koto (intermittent phrases) · Coastal cliff wind (field recording) · Distant ocean rumble (felt not heard) · Sparse piano (at dusk/star moments)
"Awe, slightly tense — standing at the edge of everything; beauty and vertigo together."
Builds slowly over session duration. Must feel like a long-exposure photograph. Visual trigger: stars appearing at dusk → music swells subtly. Hour-long ambient variant recommended.
Chapter 06 · Gold
Glowing Lighthouse
輝く灯台 (Kagayaku tōdai) — BGMKey.CHAPTER_6
BPM 64
Key D major
Non-loop
Instruments: Solo piano (start) · Acoustic guitar (mid) · Koto (approaching climax) · Wind chimes (climax) · Ocean swells (always) · Ambient pads (builds from silence) · Distant bell (finale only)
"Emotional climax — from quiet arrival to radiant restoration; every instrument earned."
Dynamic, non-looping. Each instrument added only when earned. Lighthouse activation preceded by 2s near-silence. Ambient fallback loop (ocean + sparse piano) for extended play before finale.

Section 05

Special BGM Scenes

Level Complete Sting
Duration: 4–6s · BPM: 72 · Key: D major · Non-looping · Gate: Alpha

A brief stinger played on the level-clear screen. Warm accomplishment without triumph — this is not a fanfare, it is a quiet "yes." The player did something good, not something epic.

0.0s
Acoustic guitar begins D major arpeggio ascending (D→F#→A→D')
0.8s
Soft piano joins on final note, gentle chord hold
1.5s
Single wind chime accent at peak note
2.0–5.0s
Sustained chord decays naturally to silence. No hard cutoff.
Restoration Ceremony Sting
Duration: 8–12s · BPM: 60 · Key: G major · Non-looping · Per-chapter variants · Gate: Beta

Triggered once per chapter when the island area restoration animation completes. Instruments are added with each chapter — the sound of the island growing more alive. Chapter 1 restoration is the simplest; Chapter 6 is the fullest.

0–2s
Solo koto — single sustained note, like a question
2–4s
Piano joins with a soft chord response
4–6s
Acoustic guitar enters with a gentle melodic phrase
6–9s
Nature ambience rises: birdsong, wind, ocean (chapter-dependent)
9s peak
Full gentle ensemble at emotional peak — strings of instruments present, not loud
9–12s
Decay to silence. Last sound: a single wind chime.
Chapter variants: Ch1 = Solo guitar + wind chime only · Ch2 = Koto + guitar · Ch3 = Piano + guitar + birdsong · Ch4 = Piano + guitar + ambient pads · Ch5 = Full ensemble minus bell · Ch6 = Full ensemble + distant bell (triggers lighthouse_final)
Lighthouse Finale
Duration: 20–30s · BPM: 64 · Key: D major · Non-looping · One-time play per player lifetime · Gate: Gold

This plays ONCE — the moment the lighthouse illuminates for the first time. Every second must be earned. This is the concept of 物の哀れ (mono no aware) — the bittersweet beauty of transience — rendered in sound. Preceding silence (2s) is mandatory.

-2s
[Near-silence — last BGM has faded] All ambient reduces to almost nothing
0–5s
Solo piano, intimate and sparse. Three notes, widely spaced. Like a held breath.
5–10s
Koto joins — interweaves with piano. Two voices in conversation.
10–15s
Ocean ambience rises — the island itself is joining. The sea has always been there.
12–15s
Acoustic guitar enters with the main theme's melody. Recognition.
15–18s
Wind chimes added. Ambient pads swell. The full island sound.
18–22s
Full ensemble at peak — every instrument together, quietly radiant. Not loud, but complete.
22–25s
Distant bell resonates — single clear tone, long decay. Everything else falls away around it.
25–28s
Ocean ambience alone. The bell's echo fading. Silence approaches from the right.
Production note: This piece should be recorded as a standalone musical work, then integrated. It should work without any game context — as music, it must be complete. The bell note must ring at exactly the visual moment the lighthouse beam sweeps across the island for the first time.
Section 06

UI Sound Design — 12 Micro-Sounds

UI sounds are the texture of daily interaction. Each must feel organic, never synthetic. At high repetition (button taps, dialogue advances), they must be inoffensive. At key moments (daily reward, chapter unlock), they must briefly delight.

# Key Event Feel Duration Priority Gate
01 ui_button_tap Button pressed Soft organic wooden click (~400Hz). Warm, tactile, never harsh. 60ms Medium Alpha
02 ui_menu_open Menu/panel opens Paper slide whoosh. Like unfolding a hand-drawn map. Organic texture. 200ms Low Alpha
03 ui_menu_close Menu/panel closes Reverse of menu_open. Pitch-down tail. Folding away. 180ms Low Alpha
04 ui_shop_item_select Shop item highlighted Light metallic shimmer. High pitch bend up. Small coin spinning to rest. 300ms Medium Alpha
05 ui_dialogue_advance Dialogue text advances Soft typewriter tick, 30ms, ±2st pitch variance to avoid robotic feel. Very low volume. 30ms Low Alpha
06 ui_error_cannot_do Invalid action Soft hollow low tap (~180Hz). Gentle 'no' — not a scolding. NEVER a buzzer. 80ms Medium Alpha
07 ui_notification_badge Notification badge appears Single C5 chime, 400ms decay. Soft attack. Like a distant doorbell. 400ms Low Alpha
08 ui_island_map_open Island map opens Ambient swell in. Ocean breath. Like opening a door to the outside. 600ms Medium Alpha
09 ui_chapter_unlock Chapter unlocked Rising shimmer 400ms + soft chime peak. Pentatonic fragment. Wonder, not excitement. 400ms High Alpha
10 ui_material_collect Craft material collected Small water-drop plop. Smooth pebble into calm water. Physical, organic, immediate. 120ms Medium Alpha
11 ui_daily_reward_open Daily reward opened Warm pad swell + guitar strum. Major chord. Morning light arriving. A small moment of care. 800ms Medium Alpha
12 ui_hinata_moment ひなた NPC moment Single note, soft piano or koto (E4/A4). No attack. Intimate, personal, quiet. She is right beside you. 800ms High Beta
Section 07

Voice & Narration Style

No full voice acting. This is a deliberate creative choice, not a budget constraint. Silence, ambient swells, and 1–2 syllable vocalizations carry more emotional weight than performance. The player's imagination completes each character's voice. What is not said is more powerful than what is.
Voice System Principles
  • No full sentences ever voiced
  • Max 2 syllables per character vocalization
  • All narrative carried by text + ambient music
  • Music swells during emotional text moments
  • Murmur style: Japanese wordless minimal
Narration & Journal

律's internal monologue: text only, no audio. Journal reading (丸山渉's letters): text only, music swells. Dialogue advance: ui_dialogue_advance SFX + optional character vocalization. Chapter reflection texts: ambient BGM layer rises softly.

山田律 (Yamada Ritz)
Player character · Protagonist · No voice
Audio approach: Text only for all inner monologue. Ambient music swells on key reflection moments.
Chapter complete: Optional faint breath exhale or implied 'ん…' via text+ambient. Never a full vocalization. Silence is the character's voice.
ひなた (Hinata) Beta
Island companion · 1–2 syllables max
律さん… うん ふふ
Recorded breathy, warm, close-mic. Never projected. Feels like the person next to you speaking, not performing. Triggers ui_hinata_moment simultaneously.
磯部健三 (Isobe Kenzō) Beta
Harbor fisherman · Gruff warmth
はは
Gruff warm laugh. Low, resonant, chest voice. Warm despite roughness. Used sparingly — only when truly earned. Like a boat settling on calm water.
桐島梅子 (Kirishima Umeko) Beta
Pottery studio · Contented craftsperson
[wordless hum]
Wordless contented hum — no specific syllables. Mid-range, calm, slightly raspy with age. Like someone who has found exactly their place in the world.
海野夏帆 (Umino Natsho) Beta
Forest botanist · Curious · Excitable
あっ
Bright surprised exclamation when discovering rare plants. Higher register. Genuine, not performed. The sound of real discovery.
丸山冬子 (Maruyama Fuyuko) Gold
Stone path hermit · Near-silence
…ありがとう
This single vocalization triggers ONCE in the entire game. Almost a whisper. Elderly, fragile-sounding but not weak. Players who reach it will feel it.
丸山渉 (Maruyama Wataru) Gold
Absent · Present only through journals
No voice. Ever.
Journal entries are text-only. During reading, ambient music swells. The swelling ambient music IS his voice. Absence is presence. His silence says everything.

AudioManager.ts Integration Notes

The existing AudioManager.ts handles single-source BGM with tween-based fade (via playBGM(key, fadeDuration)) and one-shot SFX (via playSFX(key)). Chapter BGM is accessible via playChapterBGM(chapterId).

Beta sprint requirement: The 3-layer adaptive system requires extending AudioManager to support multiple concurrent BGM sources or a new AdaptiveBGMManager class. Current single _bgmSource cannot handle simultaneous base + melody + intensity layers.

All SFX keys confirmed against SFXKey enum. All BGM keys confirmed against BGMKey enum. File path convention: audio/bgm/{key} and audio/sfx/{key}.
